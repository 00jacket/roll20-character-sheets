Basic Moves:


Bad guys are scary! Why stick around and see if the bad guy wants to share your pizza? Run! Roll +Agi. 10+ You out run your pursuer. 7-9 You out run your friends.


Trying to hide a clue from the bad guy? Why not try a shell game? To bluff, roll +Cha. 10+ Choose 2 from the list. 7-9 Choose 1 from the list.

  • The bluff is effective.
  • There are no personal repercussions for the bluff.
  • No one else experiences repercussions for the bluff.
  • The bluff is timely.


You intimidate someone into either doing something they wouldn’t normally do, or giving you something. You may intimidate by . . .

  • Being pushy (+Tough),
  • Sleight of hand (+Agi),
  • Spouting psesudoscience (+Int),
  • Being so hot you’re out of their league (+Cha), or
  • Doing something really weird (+Weird).
10+ Choose 2 from the list.
7-9 Choose 1 from the list.
  • They don’t resent you.
  • They run away.
  • They give you what you want.
  • They don’t try to trick you.


Sometimes you need people outside the gang to do things for you. Manipulation requires Leverage. To manipulate, roll +Cha.
10+ Choose 2 from the list.
7-9 Choose 1 from the list.

  • They act immediately.
  • They don’t need something in exchange.
  • They do it well.
  • They are happy with the bargain.


You have a suspect right where you want ‘em. Time to hit the suspect with some hard questions! Roll +Tough.
10+ Choose 2 from the list.
7-9 Choose 1 from the list.

  • Is the suspect telling the truth?
  • How could I get the suspect to ______?
  • What does the suspect want?
  • How could I get the suspect to do what I want?


This mystery isn’t going to solve itself. Time to search for clues! Roll +Int.
10+ You find a clue. Tell the Animator what it means.
7-9 You find a clue. The Animator tells you what it means.
6- Pick one: You find a clue but you might misunderstand it. Or, you don’t find a clue.


Once per episode, any member of the gang may use these. Smelling salts revive a character that has passed out from fear and cure 2 fear points.


You have enough clues to nail this bad guy. Capturing a bad guy requires 2 things: a successfully executed plan and someone to explain the mystery. Have a player make a plan. They roll 2D6 unmodified to execute it:
10+ You ably trap the bad guy. A player must use “It was Old Man Winters!” to end the mystery.
7-9 The bad guy is hindered, but still mobile. The players must escape a chase and try again. If the bad guy has already been hindered, they are captured.

  • Why they did it.
  • Where the treasure is (if there is treasure).
